English is a global language used in communication, education, business, and technology. For vocational students, mastering English enhances career opportunities and international understanding.

Grade 10: Basic Communication

1. Greetings and Introductions

Students learn to greet others, introduce themselves, and ask simple questions.

  • "Hello, my name is..."
  • "Nice to meet you."
  • "Where are you from?"

2. Daily Activities

Using simple present tense to describe routines and habits:

I go to school at 7 a.m.
She studies English every day.

3. Descriptive Text

Describing people, places, or things using adjectives:

  • "My classroom is big and clean."
  • "The library is quiet."

Grade 11: Functional English

1. Making Appointments

Students practice setting and confirming appointments:

  • "Can we meet at 3 p.m.?"
  • "I'm available on Monday."

2. Giving Instructions

Using imperative sentences:

Open your book.
Turn on the computer.
Please be quiet.

3. Narrative Text

Understanding stories with past tense usage:

Yesterday, I visited the museum.
We saw many historical items.

Tip:

Practice English every day by watching movies, reading articles, or speaking with friends.

Exercise:

Write 5 sentences about your daily routine in English.
